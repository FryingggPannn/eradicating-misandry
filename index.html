Eradicating Misandry

Semester 2: Interdependence and Equity Research Project

In many countries around the world, misandry—the hatred of or prejudice against men—is a noticeable problem, socially and legally.

  • What/Who is affected by this problem? Men and boys everywhere face discrimination.
  • Where is this problem occurring? It can occur anywhere and in any way, even in more developed countries.
  • The current situation of this problem is? Misogyny—the hatred of or prejudice against women—is also a similar issue, but much more is done to it, because many know that women have been traditionally oppressed in the past and do not accept the idea of the opposite also happening in the world. Little know about and care about the issue of misandry.
  • The ideal situation would be? Ideally, we would want a state of gender equality, where everyone, regardless of gender, are given the same opportunities and respect.
  • Potential consequences if not changed? Misandry decreases victims’ self-esteem and creates a plethora of mental health issues. Victims are also abused physically. Repeated cases of abuse like this may lead men and boys to feel or even be useless in society, removing important perspectives and perhaps even some life-changing ideas.
